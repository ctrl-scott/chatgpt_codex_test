Word, Definition, and Historical Snippet (A–Z)

  1. A – Automobile
    Definition: A self-propelled vehicle used for land transport.
    History: The Ford Model T, launched in 1908, revolutionized American transportation and mass production.
  2. B – Broadcast
    Definition: The transmission of audio or video content to a dispersed audience.
    History: The first radio broadcast took place in 1906, with commercial stations emerging in the 1920s.
  3. C – Civil Rights
    Definition: The rights of citizens to political and social freedom and equality.
    History: The American Civil Rights Movement peaked in the 1960s, led by figures like Martin Luther King Jr.
  4. D – Depression
    Definition: A severe and prolonged downturn in economic activity.
    History: The Great Depression began with the 1929 stock market crash and lasted through the 1930s.
  5. E – E-commerce
    Definition: Commercial transactions conducted electronically on the internet.
    History: Online shopping gained traction in the 1990s with websites like Amazon and eBay.
  6. F – Feminism
    Definition: The advocacy of women's rights on the basis of equality of the sexes.
    History: Second-wave feminism in the 1960s fought for workplace rights and reproductive freedom.
  7. G – Globalization
    Definition: The process by which businesses and cultures expand internationally.
    History: The post-Cold War era saw an acceleration of globalization through trade and digital networks.
  8. H – Holocaust
    Definition: The genocide of European Jews and others by Nazi Germany during WWII.
    History: From 1941 to 1945, over six million Jews were murdered in one of history's greatest atrocities.
  9. I – Internet
    Definition: A global system of interconnected computer networks.
    History: The internet became widely accessible in the 1990s, transforming communication and commerce.
  10. J – Jazz
    Definition: A genre of music with roots in African American communities.
    History: Jazz emerged in the early 20th century and influenced global music and culture.
  11. K – Korean War
    Definition: A war between North and South Korea from 1950 to 1953.
    History: The war ended in an armistice, not a peace treaty, and the Korean peninsula remains divided.
  12. L – LGBTQ+
    Definition: An acronym for lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, queer/questioning, and others.
    History: The 1969 Stonewall Riots were a catalyst for LGBTQ+ rights activism in the United States.
  13. M – Moon Landing
    Definition: The event of humans landing on the moon.
    History: In 1969, Apollo 11 made history as the first manned moon landing, with Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin.
  14. N – Nuclear
    Definition: Relating to the energy released during nuclear fission or fusion.
    History: The atomic bomb was first used in WWII, marking the start of the nuclear age.
  15. O – Occupy
    Definition: To take up space or control, often in protest.
    History: The Occupy Wall Street movement in 2011 protested economic inequality and corporate influence.
  16. P – Pandemic
    Definition: A global outbreak of a disease.
    History: The COVID-19 pandemic, declared in 2020, significantly altered global health, travel, and economics.
  17. Q – Quarantine
    Definition: A period of isolation to prevent disease spread.
    History: Mass quarantines were enforced worldwide during the COVID-19 pandemic to limit transmission.
  18. R – Riot
    Definition: A violent disturbance by a crowd.
    History: The 1992 Los Angeles riots followed the acquittal of police officers involved in the beating of Rodney King.
  19. S – Space Shuttle
    Definition: A reusable spacecraft for transporting astronauts and cargo.
    History: NASA’s first space shuttle launch occurred in 1981; the program ended in 2011.
  20. T – Television
    Definition: A system for transmitting visual images and sound.
    History: TV became a household fixture in the 1950s, reshaping news, entertainment, and culture.
  21. U – Union
    Definition: An organized association of workers for collective bargaining.
    History: The labor union movement gained traction in the early 20th century, especially after the New Deal.
  22. V – Vietnam War
    Definition: A conflict in Vietnam, Laos, and Cambodia from 1955 to 1975.
    History: U.S. involvement escalated in the 1960s; the war ended with North Vietnamese victory.
  23. W – Watergate
    Definition: A political scandal that led to U.S. President Nixon’s resignation.
    History: The 1972 break-in and cover-up unraveled over two years, revealing abuses of power.
  24. X – X-ray
    Definition: A form of electromagnetic radiation used in imaging.
    History: Medical use of X-rays expanded dramatically throughout the 20th century.
  25. Y – Youth Culture
    Definition: The behaviors and norms of young people, often distinct from mainstream society.
    History: Youth culture exploded post-WWII, with each generation reshaping fashion, music, and values.
  26. Z – Zoom
    Definition: A video communication platform.
    History: Zoom usage skyrocketed during the COVID-19 pandemic, symbolizing remote work and digital life.
